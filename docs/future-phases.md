> **Timeframe:** Month 3 and beyond

## Phase 4: Speaking Revenue System (Month 3–6)

### Speaking Page Requirements
- JSB's positioning statement (adapted from brand strategy)
- 3–5 signature talk titles with one-paragraph descriptions
- Audience types (corporate, leadership, entrepreneurship, tech, etc.)
- Social proof: past engagements, logos, testimonials
- Inquiry form (Name, Company, Event type, Date, Budget range)
- Booking process: form → auto-reply → JSB team review → Calendly call

### Speaker CRM Pipeline

```
STAGES:
  Inquiry Received
  → Initial Response Sent
  → Discovery Call Scheduled
  → Proposal Sent
  → Contract Negotiation
  → Booked + Confirmed
  → Event Complete
  → Follow-up / Testimonial Requested
```

### Speaking Fee Structure (suggested tiers)
- **Keynote (60–90 min):** Tier pricing based on org size and event type
- **Workshop (half/full day):** Flat fee + travel
- **Panel / Virtual:** Reduced rate
- **Book bundle deals:** Bulk book purchase unlocks discounted or free appearance

### Outreach Automation
- Monthly email to `speaker-prospect` segment: new talk title, recent press hit, availability
- LinkedIn outreach sequence (manual, assisted by AI drafts) to event organizers
- Follow up with past inquiries who did not convert: 30, 60, 90-day cadence

## Phase 5: Sponsorship + Brand Partnerships (Month 4+)

### Sponsorship Assets to Build
1. **Media Kit** — Audience demographics, list size, engagement rates, reach
2. **Sponsor Deck** — Packages, placements, pricing, case for alignment with JSB's brand
3. **Brand Alignment Criteria** — What JSB will and won't endorse (protects brand integrity)

### Sponsorship Packages (template)

| Package | Placement | Frequency | Price |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Newsletter Sponsor | Dedicated section in weekly email | Monthly | $X |
| Substack Feature | Sponsored post or integration | Per issue | $X |
| Podcast Sponsor | Pre-roll + mid-roll mention | Per episode | $X |
| Speaking Sponsor | Event co-branding | Per event | $X |
| Annual Partner | Multi-channel, co-created content | Annual | $X |

**Note:** Pricing set once list size and engagement benchmarks are confirmed. Rule of thumb: $30–50 CPM for high-engagement creator newsletters.

### Sponsor Outreach Automation
- Make/Zapier: Track sponsor inquiry form → CRM → notification
- Follow-up sequence: 3 emails over 21 days if no response to initial deck
- Quarterly media kit update (auto-remind via recurring task)

## Phase 6: Digital Products + Courses (Month 6+)

### Product Ladder

```
FREE → PAID (Low) → PAID (Mid) → PAID (High) → 1:1
  │          │            │             │          │
Quiz    Toolkit/     Online         Cohort    Advisory
       Workbook     Course       Mastermind  Retainer
  $0    $27–$47    $197–$497    $1,500–$5k   $X/month
```

### Launch Sequence for Each Product
1. Seed the idea in Substack (gauge interest via comments/responses)
2. Survey the list (3-question email: "Would you buy X?")
3. Build waitlist (separate CRM tag)
4. Create product
5. Beta launch to waitlist at discount
6. Full launch to list
7. Evergreen via email nurture → sales page

### Course Delivery Infrastructure
- **Platform:** Kajabi, Teachable, or Podia (Kajabi preferred for integrated email + course + community)
- **Integration:** Connect Kajabi to CRM for purchase tagging
- **Community:** Circle.so or Kajabi Community for cohort/mastermind

## The 10 Non-Negotiable Principles for JSB's Brand

1. **One voice, all channels.** Everything sounds like JSB — never generic.
2. **Human Mode is a lens, not just a book title.** Every piece of content sees the world through it.
3. **Own the list.** Social platforms rent audiences; the email list is the asset.
4. **Depth over volume.** A smaller, highly-engaged list beats a large cold one every time.
5. **Every automation should feel personal.** Set it up right once; the subscriber never knows it's automated.
6. **PR amplifies what already exists.** Build the platform first; media validates it.
7. **Data informs, instinct decides.** Use analytics to remove bad ideas, not to generate good ones.
8. **Revenue follows relevance.** Stay relevant, and monetization follows naturally.
9. **Protect the brand.** Say no to partnerships that dilute the positioning.
10. **Play the long game.** A book launch is a sprint inside a marathon. Build for 10 years.
