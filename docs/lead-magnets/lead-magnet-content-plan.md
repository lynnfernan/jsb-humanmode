## Asset Briefs

---

### Asset 3 — The Human Mode Manifesto

**Format:** Short-form, shareable declaration. No gate.

**Purpose:** Brand awareness. Social sharing. Identity signal for JSB's reader.

**Concept:** A one-page (digital or printable) declaration of what it means to be in Human Mode. Not a list of tactics. A statement of belief. Something people share because it says something about them.

**Structure:**
- 10–12 statements, each 1–2 lines
- Written in second person or declaration form
- Ends with: "This is Human Mode. Choose it."

**Sample lines:**
- "You believe your judgment is worth developing — not just your output."
- "You can sit in a difficult conversation without filling the silence."
- "You are not afraid of the machines. You are more interested in yourself."
- "You know the difference between the urgent and the important — and you act accordingly."
- "You lead the actual room you're in, not the one you're planning for."

**Distribution:**
- Downloadable PDF linked from website footer and About page
- End of every Substack essay: "If this resonates, the Manifesto is yours."
- Shared as image post on launch week
- Included in welcome sequence email 3

---

### Asset 4 — The Human Mode Score

**Format:** 10–15 question assessment. 4 persona outputs. Email gate before result.

**Public Title:** What's Your Human Mode Score?
**Subtitle:** A 5-minute assessment that shows where your human advantage is strongest — and where it needs the most attention.

**Hook:**
> Machines are getting better at almost everything. The question isn't whether they'll outperform you at tasks. It's whether you've developed the things they cannot replicate.
>
> Five minutes. Honest answers. You'll know where you actually stand.
>
> → [Take the assessment]

**The 4 Persona Outputs:**

| Persona | Strength | Development Edge |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| The Sentinel | Reads situations and people with precision | Translates observation into connection |
| The Architect | Builds frameworks, systems, and strategy | Lets the unexpected in |
| The Catalyst | Activates others and creates momentum | Slows down for depth |
| The Anchor | Creates safety, trust, and continuity | Expands range and risk tolerance |

**Scoring dimensions (maps to 4 book elements):**
1. Social Emotional Aperture — how wide is your lens?
2. Bricolaging — how creatively do you combine resources?
3. Strategic Competent Humility — how well do you lead through uncertainty?
4. Quiet Understanding — how attuned are you to emotional data?

**Result page structure:**
- Your score + primary persona name
- One-paragraph read on what this means
- Your strongest dimension
- Your development edge
- One practice to start this week (from the 25 Practices)
- Secondary persona noted
- CTA: "This is the foundation of Human Mode. Here's the book that builds on it." → [Book link]

**Email gate microcopy:**
> Your score is ready.
> Where should we send it?
>
> (We'll also send you Jeffrey's monthly note — the one piece of leadership writing that doesn't read like everything else. Unsubscribe anytime.)

**CRM tags on completion:** `assessment:human-mode-score`, `persona:[sentinel/architect/catalyst/anchor]`, `score:[1-100]`

---

### Asset 5a–5d — Persona Playbooks

**Format:** PDF, 8–12 pages. Delivered via email. Named per persona.

**Purpose:** Depth content that creates genuine value before the book purchase. The reader who gets a playbook is already living the JSB framework — the book is the continuation.

**Structure (each playbook):**
1. Your persona at a glance (1 page)
2. The situation you're built for (1 page)
3. The situation that stretches you (1 page)
4. Three practices from Human Mode you can use this week (3 pages — one practice each)
5. One question to sit with (half page)
6. What comes next: Human Mode the book (half page)

**Practice selection per persona:**
- Sentinel: Practices #5 (Widen Your Aperture), #18 (Resist Dismissing or Fixing), #21 (Indirectly Acknowledge Emotions)
- Architect: Practices #16 (Highlight Parts Unknown), #15 (Insist on Low-Fidelity First), #13 (Never Accept a Lone Idea)
- Catalyst: Practices #4 (Allow for Serendipity), #8 (Mingle Better), #14 (Celebrate a Full Trash Bin)
- Anchor: Practices #11 (Bind Psychological Safety), #17 (Pull Emotions Out of the Vault), #22 (Create Space for Expressions)

---

### Asset 6 — The Human Mode Framework Worksheet

**Format:** Single-page PDF. Clean, visual, printable.

**Purpose:** The framework made tangible. Something a leader can put on their desk, use in a 1:1, or share with their team.

**Content:**
- The 4 elements as quadrants
- 3–4 questions per quadrant to self-assess
- One "practice this week" line per quadrant
- The Human Mode URL at the bottom

**Delivery:**
- Exclusive to email list first (launch week)
- Available as gated download on website after launch week
- Referenced in welcome sequence email 5
