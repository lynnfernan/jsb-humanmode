**Subtitle:** A 3-minute diagnostic for leaders who suspect they're missing something.

## Hook Copy

### LinkedIn / Substack CTA

Most leaders don't fail because they lose focus.

They fail because they focus too well — on the wrong thing.

Every leader has an altimeter. The instrument they trust most. The signal they check first. The number, the room, the fire, the approval, the arc.

It's not a flaw. It's how you got here.

But every altimeter has a blindspot. And the tighter your lock, the wider the blindspot gets.

Take 3 minutes to find out what you're locked on — and what you're missing because of it.

## The 5 Lock Types

| Type | The Fixation | The Blindspot |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| The Metric Magnet | The number on the dashboard | What the number is no longer measuring |
| The Output Optimizer | Shipping, closing, finishing | Whether the team is still standing |
| The Approval Tracker | How it's landing with people above you | What the people beside you are signaling |
| The Fire Fighter | The urgent, the broken, the loud | The slow erosion no one is naming |
| The Vision Holder | The future, the strategy, the arc | The actual room you're in right now |

## The 10 Questions

> **Format note:** Scenario-based, not Likert scales. Each question presents a realistic moment. Each answer maps to a lock type. The Scan option (F) appears on questions where it is most diagnostic — it maps to the "Scan Reserve" tally, not a lock type.

---

**Q1.** It's Friday afternoon. Your team just hit the quarterly target. The first thing you notice is:

- A) The number — and how close we came to missing it. `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) That two people on the team look completely fried. `[Output Optimizer blindspot — scan indicator]`
- C) Whether your boss has seen the result yet. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) The three things still on fire that no one's looking at. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) What this means for next quarter's narrative. `[Vision Holder]`

> *Scoring note: Option B is the "scan" answer on this question — selecting it across questions reveals someone NOT primarily locked. Handle in scoring.*

---

**Q2.** Monday morning. You open your inbox before the week officially starts. The first thing you actually scan for is:

- A) Any number that's moved — up or down — since Friday. `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) What needs a response today so nothing falls behind. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) Whether anyone important has replied to something you sent. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) What's on fire that no one's flagged yet. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Whether anything changes your read on where the quarter is heading. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Honestly — you're looking for who needs something from you that you haven't noticed yet. `[Scan]`

---

**Q3.** A project you're responsible for misses a milestone. No one's panicking yet. Your first instinct is to:

- A) Pull the numbers — where exactly did it fall short, and by how much? `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) Figure out what needs to happen in the next 48 hours to recover. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) Think about how you'll frame this for your manager before they hear about it. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Start clearing your calendar — this is about to need your full attention. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Step back and ask whether this milestone was the right one in the first place. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Call the person closest to the work and just listen. `[Scan]`

---

**Q4.** A direct report tells you, "I'm doing fine, just a lot going on." You:

- A) Take it at face value and refocus on the deliverable. `[Output Optimizer]`
- B) Run a quick mental check: are they hitting their numbers? `[Metric Magnet]`
- C) Wonder if you've been too demanding lately. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Add it to the list of things to circle back to when the current crisis ends. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Make a note to talk through it at your next 1:1 — once you've thought through what they need to hear. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Pause the conversation right there. Something just didn't sound right. `[Scan]`

---

**Q5.** You're in a 1:1 with a direct report. They're answering your questions, hitting their talking points. But something nags at you. Most likely, it's:

- A) A number they mentioned that doesn't quite add up. `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) They haven't mentioned the deliverable that's due this week. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) You can't tell if they actually respect your input or are just going through the motions. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Something in what they said feels like it's about to become a problem. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) They seem focused on execution but not on the bigger picture of where they're going. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Their energy. Something in the room doesn't match what they're saying. `[Scan]`

---

**Q6.** Your manager gives you feedback that's hard to read — not negative, but not quite positive either. You walk out of the meeting and:

- A) Replay the conversation for any specific data points — what exactly did they measure? `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) Make a list of what you can show them before the next check-in. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) Spend the next hour trying to decode what it meant about where you stand. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Decide to fix anything that could be a problem before it becomes one. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Start drafting the narrative you'd want them to have about your work. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Sit with it. Unclear feedback usually tells you something about the relationship, not just the work. `[Scan]`

---

**Q7.** It's been a genuinely hard week. You're heading home Friday evening. The thought that keeps surfacing is:

- A) Whether the numbers are where they need to be. `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) Everything still on your list that you didn't get to. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) Whether you came across the way you wanted to in that difficult conversation. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) The thing that's going to blow up Monday morning if you don't catch it now. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Whether any of this week's chaos actually moved you toward anything that matters. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) One person on your team — not what they did, just how they seemed. `[Scan]`

---

**Q8.** You're preparing for a high-stakes leadership or board meeting. The night before, you spend the most time on:

- A) Stress-testing the numbers so nothing catches you off guard. `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) Making sure every deliverable you committed to is accounted for. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) Anticipating every question so you're never caught flat-footed. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Identifying the one issue that might derail the conversation — and prepping a response. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Sharpening the narrative arc — what story are you actually trying to tell? `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Thinking about who's in the room and what each person actually needs to hear. `[Scan]`

---

**Q9.** You're in a meeting and notice the energy in the room has dropped. Your move:

- A) Press forward — we have an agenda to finish. `[Output Optimizer]`
- B) Check the clock; we're behind. `[Metric Magnet]`
- C) Wonder if you said something wrong. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Wait to see if anyone raises an issue you need to address. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Reframe the meeting toward the bigger picture to re-energize them. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Stop and ask the room: "Something just shifted. What's going on?" `[Scan]`

> *Scoring note: Q9 is the tiebreaker question. If two lock types are tied, the result defaults to the type matching the Q9 answer.*

---

**Q10.** A peer — someone at your level who you respect — is visibly struggling. Not asking for help, but not okay. You:

- A) Stay out of it. They know their numbers better than you do. `[Metric Magnet]`
- B) Offer to take something off their plate. That's the most concrete help you can give. `[Output Optimizer]`
- C) Hold back. You don't want to overstep or make it weird. `[Approval Tracker]`
- D) Flag it quietly to someone who has the authority to do something. `[Fire Fighter]`
- E) Think through what advice you'd give if they asked — and wait for the right moment to share it. `[Vision Holder]`
- F) Find a quiet moment, sit down next to them, and say: "Hey. How are you actually doing?" `[Scan]`

---

## Scoring Logic

**Tallying:**
- Each non-Scan answer = 1 point to that lock type.
- Scan answers (F options) = 1 point to the Scan Reserve tally.

**Result rules:**
1. Highest type total = **primary lock**
2. Second-highest = **secondary lock** (named in result copy)
3. If Scan Reserve ≥ 4, result is reframed as the **Scan result** — primary type still named, but framed as drift, not default
4. If two lock types tie, the tiebreaker goes to the type matching the **Q9 answer**

### Result: The Metric Magnet

**You're a Metric Magnet.**

The number on the dashboard is your altimeter. You read it before coffee. You know what it was last week, last month, last quarter. You can feel a half-percent drift before the system flags it.

This is a strength. Most leaders can't see what you see.

But here's what the altimeter doesn't show: whether the number still measures the thing that matters. Whether your team trusts the number. Whether hitting it this quarter is costing you the people who'll make next quarter possible.

**Your secondary lock: Output Optimizer.** When the number is good, you push for more. When the number is bad, you push harder. Either way, you push.

**One practice for this week:**
Before you check the number tomorrow morning, name one person on your team and ask yourself: what is the number not telling me about them right now? Then go find out.

That's the Scan Routine. It doesn't replace the altimeter. It rotates around it.

### Result: The Output Optimizer

**You're an Output Optimizer.**

Shipping is your language. Closing, finishing, delivering — that's how you measure a good day. When others are still in conversation, you're already thinking about next steps. When the meeting ends, you're already drafting the recap.

This is a real skill. Many leaders can discuss forever and never actually execute. You're not one of them.

But here's what the shipping cycle can hide: whether the people doing the work are still standing. You can optimize a team into the ground without ever intending to. Output tells you what got done. It doesn't tell you what it cost.

**Your secondary lock: Metric Magnet.** When the output is high, you add more. When it drops, you accelerate. The dashboard gives you permission to push harder — or a reason to.

**One practice for this week:**
Before you send the next recap or next-steps list, pause. Name one person on the team who made this output happen. Ask yourself: what have I said to them this week that wasn't about the work?

If you can't answer, that's the scan.

### Result: The Approval Tracker

**You're an Approval Tracker.**

You read rooms. You know who the real decision-maker is before the meeting starts. You track tone, word choice, the half-second pause before someone answers. This is not paranoia — it's pattern recognition, and it makes you effective in ways most people can't explain.

The cost is where it gets complicated. When your primary signal is how you're landing with the people above you, the people beside you start transmitting on a frequency you're not tuned to. Their signals — subtle, lateral, quiet — get missed. Not because you don't care. Because you're focused.

**Your secondary lock: Fire Fighter.** When approval feels uncertain, you tend to act — fix something, preempt something, make the problem go away before it becomes a judgment about you.

**One practice for this week:**
In your next meeting, pick one person who isn't in a position to evaluate your performance. Watch them. Not for cues about how you're landing — for what they actually need from the conversation.

That shift in attention is the scan.

### Result: The Fire Fighter

**You're a Fire Fighter.**

You don't just tolerate urgency — you're calibrated to it. When something breaks, you're already moving. You triage faster than most people can process the problem. And when the smoke clears, you're usually the one who made it happen.

Organizations need people like you. The problem is that organizations will give you all the fires you can handle — and then some more.

Here's what gets lost in the smoke: the slow erosion. The things nobody's naming yet. The pattern that isn't loud enough to be a crisis but is definitely building. Your lock keeps you responsive to what's urgent. It doesn't have a mechanism for what's quiet.

**Your secondary lock: Output Optimizer.** Fighting fires is a form of shipping — closing the problem, clearing the item, moving to the next. The satisfaction of resolution is real. It's also a reinforcement loop.

**One practice for this week:**
At the end of tomorrow, before the last fire of the day pulls you in — spend five minutes asking: what has been slightly wrong for three weeks that I haven't had time to name? Write it down. Don't fix it yet. Just see it.

That's where the scan starts.

### Result: The Vision Holder

**You're a Vision Holder.**

You live in the arc. Where this is going, what it all adds up to, what the thing means — that's your native language. You can hold complexity that others find overwhelming and turn it into something people can actually follow. Ambiguity doesn't confuse you. You use it.

This is a rare and genuine strength. Strategy needs people who can see the shape of the thing before it exists.

But the arc can lift you out of the room. The people in front of you right now — not the future team, not the eventual outcome, but the actual human being sitting across from you today — that's where the gap opens. Vision can be a form of distance.

**Your secondary lock: Approval Tracker.** You're attentive to whether your vision lands. You watch for whether people buy in. The feedback loop you're monitoring is strategic resonance — not personal connection.

**One practice for this week:**
In your next 1:1, resist the reframe. When the conversation drifts toward tactics, don't pull it back toward the arc. Stay in the room. Let them lead. The thing they're actually trying to tell you is more important than the narrative you're holding.

That's the scan.

### Result: The Scanner *(Scan Reserve ≥ 4)*

**You're scanning more than most leaders do.**

Most people who take this diagnostic land clearly in one type. You didn't — which is itself diagnostic. You're not fixed on one altimeter. You move between them. And you notice when something doesn't fit the instrument at all.

Here's the honest read: scanning is a skill. Staying present, reading the room, noticing what the metrics don't capture — these aren't soft instincts. They're a form of intelligence that most leadership cultures actively train out of people.

But scanning without an anchor is its own risk. At your best, you integrate everything. At your most stretched, you're responsive to everything — which can feel like you're nowhere.

**Your drift lock: [Primary type name].** When the pressure builds and the scanning gets harder, this is where you tend to land. Not your default — your escape. Know the difference.

**One practice for this week:**
Notice when you stop scanning. Not the result — the moment. What was happening when the altimeter took over? That's the data that matters.

## Implementation Notes

**Quiz platform:** Typeform, Scoreapp, or any platform supporting weighted scoring and conditional result pages.

**Scoring setup:**
- Create 5 score buckets: `metric_magnet`, `output_optimizer`, `approval_tracker`, `fire_fighter`, `vision_holder`
- Create 1 scan reserve bucket: `scan`
- Each answer maps exactly one point to one bucket
- Logic: show result page matching highest bucket; surface second-highest as secondary lock in the copy
- Scan override: if `scan ≥ 4`, redirect to Scanner result page regardless of highest type bucket (pass primary type into the copy dynamically)
- Tiebreaker: if top two buckets are equal, use Q9 answer to determine primary

**Email integration:**
- Gate fires before result page is shown
- Tag subscribers at capture with their result type (e.g., `lock-type:metric-magnet`)
- Use tag to trigger the correct result email in CRM
- First email in sequence: result summary + the one weekly practice, personalized by type

**Design priority:**
The share card is the highest-leverage design asset in this entire asset. One card per type. Get these right before anything else.
