## The Concept

**Human Mode, Always: The Experience** is not a conference. It is not a keynote with cocktails.

It is a full-sensory journey through the core ideas of *MATTERING* — designed so that every guest *feels* the concepts in their body before they understand them in their mind.

From the moment they arrive to the moment they leave, guests move through a series of activations that mirror the book's arc: the trap of tunnel vision, the shock of expanded awareness, the practice of the Scan Routine, and the recognition that they are not — and have never been — falling alone.

**Target vibe:** Immersive art installation meets TED-caliber intellectual experience meets high-production LA bling. Think Meow Wolf meets Accenture Innovation Lab meets a skydiving drop zone that someone made impossibly cool.

---

## Guest Journey Overview

```
ARRIVAL                    THE EXPERIENCE              DEPARTURE
─────────                  ──────────────              ─────────
Freefall Entry      →    Six Activation Zones    →   The Landing
(atmospheric)            (book pillars)               (branded takeaway
                                                       + community moment)
```

Each zone corresponds to a core pillar from *MATTERING*. Guests move through at their own pace — there is no prescribed order after the entry. The full scan is the point.

## Zone-by-Zone Activations

---

### ZONE 0 — The Freefall Entry
**Concept:** *Altimeter Lock begins at the door*

The entry tunnel is the first disorientation. Guests step into a 20-foot immersive corridor with:

- **Floor-to-ceiling projection** of a sky rushing upward — guests feel like they are in freefall before the event has even started
- **Spatial audio:** wind, the distant hum of an aircraft, then silence
- **On the walls:** a single line in oversized Sofia Sans Bold in Navy on Cream —
  > *"At 180 mph, the instinct is to fixate. What you focus on next changes everything."*
- **Transition moment:** the tunnel opens into the full event space, which is wide, lit in warm cream and slate blue, and deliberately overwhelming in the best way — there is too much to see at once. That's the point.

**AV Spec:** Christie laser projectors (floor-to-ceiling, 180°), d&b audiotechnik spatial audio, custom wind soundscape.

---

### ZONE 1 — The Altimeter Lock Chamber
**Concept:** *Feel what tunnel vision actually costs*

A circular room with a giant 3D altimeter at its center — 8 feet tall, backlit in Navy, analog face with a single sweeping hand. Guests enter and experience two states:

**State A — Locked:**
- The room narrows visually via dynamic lighting until only the altimeter is illuminated
- Peripheral screens go dark one by one
- Spatial audio drops teammates' voices to silence
- A single number pulses: the metric, the target, the one thing you're watching
- Guests feel the isolation of single-point focus

**State B — The Scan:**
- A hand appears (projection) — the Instructor's Gesture — pointing outward
- Lights expand, screens re-illuminate, voices return
- The room fills with the full picture: team, landscape, sky, mission
- The altimeter is still there. But it is no longer the only thing.

**Takeaway:** A custom-stamped coin with the Scan Routine on one side, the altimeter graphic on the other. *Keep it in your pocket. It is your rip cord.*

**AV Spec:** Circular LED wall (270°), theatrical lighting rig, spatial audio, custom 3D altimeter prop fabrication.

---

### ZONE 2 — The Team in Freefall
**Concept:** *You cannot see the formation from inside the fixation*

The largest zone. A high-ceiling open space anchored by:

**The Parachute Mural:**
- A floor-to-ceiling (20–25 ft) hand-painted or large-format printed mural of skydivers in formation — diverse, human, mid-jump
- Navy and Slate Blue palette, Cream sky
- Painted in a style that is cinematic and real, not cartoonish
- The formation has one open slot — the guest's slot

**The Freefall Photo Booth:**
- Guests step into a branded photo moment in front of the mural
- AR overlay option: their photo places them *inside* the formation
- Print-on-demand: branded 4x6 with their name, the date, and the line *"You are not falling alone."*
- Digital version auto-sent to phone with watermark-free sharing for social

**The Interdependence Wall:**
- A living installation: as guests add their "one thing they need others to see" on a tag, it joins hundreds of others hanging from a net suspended from the ceiling
- By end of night, the net is full — the visual proof that awareness is collective

---

### ZONE 3 — The Bricolage Lab
**Concept:** *Leadership is building with what you have, not waiting for the perfect tools*

An interactive maker-station inspired by the Bricolage leadership concept (from Raj's persona and JSB's research):

- Tables stocked with raw materials: rope, carabiners, fabric, maps, compasses, chalk
- A rotating prompt on the wall (updated every 30 min): *"With what's on this table, solve ___."*
- Not a craft project — a conversation starter about resourcefulness, instinct, and leading under constraint
- A JSB facilitator or trained host floats the room and draws out insights
- Outputs from the lab are photographed and shared on a live feed projected in the main space

**Why it works:** It makes Bricolage *tangible* for every persona — Raj recognizes himself, Marcus gets a structure for the instinct he's been running on, Elena is humbled.

---

### ZONE 4 — The Scan Routine Lounge
**Concept:** *The practice, not the theory*

The most intimate zone. Designed as a high-end lounge — deep seating, low lighting, Slate Blue and Cream, soft sound. This is where guests integrate.

**The Scan Cards:**
- Each table has a set of five oversize cards: **Altimeter / Rip Cord / Sky / Team / Landmarks**
- Conversation prompts on each card tie the step to professional and personal life
- Guests work through the Scan Routine in pairs or small groups, guided by the cards

**The Ritual Moment:**
- Every 45 minutes, a soft chime sounds throughout the event
- All zones pause for 60 seconds
- A voice (JSB, pre-recorded or live) guides the room through a single Scan Routine cycle
- Then the noise resumes

**Why it works:** The pause is felt. It models — physically, in the body — what it means to interrupt tunnel vision with grace.

---

### ZONE 5 — The Gesture Wall (Interactive Confession + Commitment)
**Concept:** *The leadership act that changes everything*

A large backlit panel — Navy with Cream text, floor-to-ceiling — with a simple prompt:

> *"What fixation have you been letting slide? What's the gesture you need to make?"*

Guests write anonymously on small cards and pin them to the wall. By end of night it becomes a collective landscape of honest leadership reflection.

**Adjacent:** A video loop of JSB delivering the core "Instructor's Gesture" story from the keynote — visceral, real, 4 minutes. Guests can watch alone or in small groups.

## The Landing — Departure Experience

The exit is earned.

As guests leave, they receive a **branded keepsake kit** in a matte Navy envelope:

- The stamped coin (from Zone 1)
- A printed copy of the Scan Routine on Cream card stock
- A QR code linking to a private post-event resource (the *MATTERING* early access chapter or assessment)
- A handwritten (or high-quality printed) note from JSB

**Final wall text at the door:**

> *"You came in fixated. You leave with the complete picture.*
> *That is Human Mode, Always.*
> *Now go interrupt some tunnel vision."*

## Visual Identity at Event Scale

- **Navy (`#1c4b61`):** All structural elements — walls, signage headers, the altimeter prop, wristbands
- **Slate Blue (`#578ead`):** Lighting wash, accent installations, card stock
- **Cream (`#f1f1e2`):** All text surfaces, breathing room in the layout, the mural sky
- **Typography:** Sofia Sans Bold for all wayfinding and zone headers; Saira for subheads and prompts
- **Minimum 40% cream/open space** in every zone — even at a busy event, the scan must feel possible

## Guest Outcomes

Every guest should leave with:

1. **A felt memory** — not just information. The freefall tunnel, the coin in their pocket, the pause ritual.
2. **A name for something they already knew** — Altimeter Lock, Bricolage, the Scan Routine.
3. **A social artifact** — the photo in the formation, shareable and branded.
4. **A next step** — the keepsake kit with a clear entry into the *MATTERING* ecosystem.
5. **A community signal** — they are part of a formation now. They are not falling alone.
